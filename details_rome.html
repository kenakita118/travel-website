Rome

Rome, the capital city of Italy, boasts a rich history that spans over 2,800 years. Legend has it that Rome was founded by Romulus and Remus in 753 BC. Initially a monarchy, it evolved into a republic around 509 BC, characterized by a system of checks and balances. The Roman Republic saw expansion across the Mediterranean, marked by the Punic Wars against Carthage. In 45 BC, Julius Caesar's crossing of the Rubicon River led to the demise of the republic, and Rome transitioned into an empire. Augustus, the first emperor, ushered in the Pax Romana, a period of relative peace and prosperity. The Roman Empire reached its zenith under Trajan in the 2nd century AD, encompassing vast territories. However, economic challenges, invasions, and internal strife contributed to its decline. In 476 AD, the Western Roman Empire fell to the Germanic chieftain Odoacer. Rome's legacy endures through its contributions to law, governance, architecture, and culture. The Roman Catholic Church, established in Rome, became a dominant force. The city experienced Renaissance revival in the 15th century, with artists like Michelangelo and Leonardo da Vinci leaving an indelible mark. Today, Rome stands as a captivating blend of ancient ruins and modern vibrancy, reflecting its enduring significance in world history.

Visa and Passport

Check visa requirements for your nationality and ensure your passport is valid for the duration of your stay.

Currency

The official currency is the Euro. Have some cash for smaller transactions, and check exchange rates.

Language

Italian is the official language, but English is widely spoken in tourist areas. Learning a few basic Italian phrases can enhance your experience.

Weather

Rome has a Mediterranean climate. Check the weather forecast and pack accordingly.

Transportation

Familiarize yourself with Rome's public transportation system, including buses and the metro. Consider getting a Roma Pass for convenience.

Sights

Plan your itinerary, including must-visit sites like the Colosseum, Roman Forum, Vatican City, and the Pantheon. Purchase tickets in advance to skip lines.

Shoes

Rome is best explored on foot. Comfortable walking shoes are essential for navigating cobblestone streets.

Local Cuisine

Enjoy Italian delicacies like pasta, pizza, gelato, and espresso. Tipping is customary but not as extensive as in some other countries.

Safety

Rome is generally safe, but be cautious of pickpockets in crowded areas. Keep valuables secure and be aware of your surroundings.

Opening Hours

Some shops and businesses may close in the afternoon for a siesta. Plan activities around local opening hours.

Closures

Check for closure days of attractions and museums to avoid disappointment.

COVID-19 Guidelines

Be aware of any Covid-19 related travel guidelines, including testing requirements and health protocols.

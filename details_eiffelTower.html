Eiffel Tower

The Eiffel Tower, one of the most iconic landmarks in the world, has a storied history dating back to its construction in the late 19th century. Completed in 1889, the tower was designed by Gustave Eiffel as the entrance arch for the 1889 World's Fair, also known as the Exposition Universelle, held in Paris to celebrate the 100th anniversary of the French Revolution. The construction of the Eiffel Tower was a monumental engineering feat, utilizing over 18,000 individual iron pieces and held together by more than two million rivets. Despite initial criticism for its design, the tower soon became a symbol of Paris and a marvel of modern engineering. Originally intended to stand for only 20 years, the Eiffel Tower was nearly dismantled in 1909. However, it was saved due to its valuable role in telecommunications, serving as a radio transmission tower. Over the years, the Eiffel Tower has become a global cultural icon, attracting millions of visitors annually who come to admire its architectural brilliance, panoramic views of Paris, and its significance in French history and culture. Today, it stands as a testament to both artistic and engineering achievement.

Opening Hours

The Eiffel Tower is generally open every day. Check the official website for the current opening hours as they may vary seasonally.

Tickets

Purchase tickets in advance to skip long lines. Tickets can be bought online or at the ticket counters on-site.

Different Levels

The tower has three levels. Consider which level(s) you want to visit – the higher you go, the more expansive the views.

Elevators and Stairs

You can take elevators or climb stairs to reach the different levels. If you're up for it, climbing the stairs can be a unique experience.

Lights at Night

The Eiffel Tower is beautifully illuminated at night. Witness the sparkling lights for a magical experience, which occurs every hour on the hour.

Picnicking

While you can't bring a picnic inside, the Champ de Mars park nearby is a great spot for a picnic with a view of the Eiffel Tower.

Weather Considerations

Paris weather can be unpredictable. Check the weather forecast and dress accordingly, especially if you plan to visit the open-air top level.

Photography

Capture the iconic moments but be aware that tripods are not allowed. Also, be cautious of pickpockets, especially in crowded areas.

Guided Tours

Consider joining a guided tour to gain insights into the history and architecture of the Eiffel Tower.

Security

There are security checks before entering, so be prepared for a brief screening process.

Proximity to Attractions

The Eiffel Tower is close to other major attractions like the Seine River, Trocadéro Gardens, and the Louvre Museum. Plan your itinerary accordingly.

Public Transportation

The Eiffel Tower is well-connected by public transportation. Consider using the metro or buses to reach the site conveniently.

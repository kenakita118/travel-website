Maldives

The Eiffel Tower, one of the most iconic landmarks in the world, has a storied history dating back to its construction in the late 19th century. Completed in 1889, the tower was designed by Gustave Eiffel as the entrance arch for the 1889 World's Fair, also known as the Exposition Universelle, held in Paris to celebrate the 100th anniversary of the French Revolution. The construction of the Eiffel Tower was a monumental engineering feat, utilizing over 18,000 individual iron pieces and held together by more than two million rivets. Despite initial criticism for its design, the tower soon became a symbol of Paris and a marvel of modern engineering. Originally intended to stand for only 20 years, the Eiffel Tower was nearly dismantled in 1909. However, it was saved due to its valuable role in telecommunications, serving as a radio transmission tower. Over the years, the Eiffel Tower has become a global cultural icon, attracting millions of visitors annually who come to admire its architectural brilliance, panoramic views of Paris, and its significance in French history and culture. Today, it stands as a testament to both artistic and engineering achievement.

Visa Requirements

Most tourists do not require a visa for stays of up to 30 days. However, it's essential to check the specific visa requirements based on your nationality.

Currency

The Maldivian Rufiyaa (MVR) is the official currency, but U.S. dollars are widely accepted. Ensure you have some local currency for smaller transactions.

Weather

The Maldives has a tropical climate, and weather conditions can vary. Check the weather forecast for your travel dates and pack accordingly.

Health Precautions

No specific vaccinations are required for entry, but it's advisable to be up-to-date on routine vaccinations. Consider health precautions for water-related activities.

Resort Bookings

Many visitors stay in luxury resorts on private islands. Ensure you have your accommodation booked in advance, and check the resort's policies, including any COVID-19-related guidelines.

Local Customs

The Maldives is a Muslim-majority country, and visitors should respect local customs. Dress modestly when outside resorts, especially in inhabited islands.

Transportation

Plan your transportation within the Maldives, whether it's by seaplane, speedboat, or domestic flights between islands.

Marine Life

The Maldives is known for its vibrant coral reefs and marine life. If you plan on snorkeling or diving, familiarize yourself with local guidelines to protect the environment.

Travel Insurance

Consider comprehensive travel insurance that covers potential medical expenses and unforeseen events.

Language

Dhivehi is the official language, but English is widely spoken, especially in tourist areas.
